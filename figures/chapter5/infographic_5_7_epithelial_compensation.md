# Infographic 5.7: Biology vs. Physics - The Epithelial Compensation Mechanism

**Visual Description:**
A cross-sectional schematic of the cornea showing the dynamic interaction between the laser ablation (Physics) and the healing response (Biology).

**Key Elements:**
1.  **The "Trough" (Physics):** The laser removes tissue in a ring pattern (annulus) to steepen the center (Hyperopic Ablation).
2.  **The "Filler" (Biology):** The epithelial layer (shown in distinct texture/color) thickens specifically inside this trough, acting like "biological putty" to smooth the surface.
3.  **The "Regression Effect":** Arrows show how this filling reduces the effective steepening, causing the eye to lose its near vision effect (Regression).
4.  **The "Strategic Overcorrection":** A dotted line showing the *deeper* initial ablation (+0.50D) which, *after* the inevitable filling, results in the perfect target curve.

**Caption:**
*Figura 5.11: O "Cabo de Guerra" biológico. O laser esculpe uma curvatura (Física), mas o epitélio tenta suavizá-la (Biologia). A estratégia de compensação (+0.50D / -0.1Q) cria um "excesso" deliberado para que, após a cicatrização, o resultado final seja o ideal.*
