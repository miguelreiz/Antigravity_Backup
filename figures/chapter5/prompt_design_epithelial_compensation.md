# Visual Design: The Epithelial Compensation Mechanism

## 1. Specialist Clinical Description
**The Phenomenon:**
In hyperopic PRK, the laser does not touch the center of the cornea. Instead, it ablates a **paracentral annulus (ring)** of tissue. This removal of tissue creates a "trough" or "moat" around the center, which effectively makes the central island of cornea steeper (increasing its refractive power for near vision).

**The Problem (Biology vs. Physics):**
The corneal epithelium (the surface "skin" cells) acts like a fluid masking agent. It follows the "Law of Smoothing".
*   It **thickens** (hyperplasia) inside the deep ablation trough where curvature changes are abrupt.
*   By filling this trough, it dampens the steepening effect created by the laser.
*   **Result:** The effective optical profile becomes smoother/flatter than intended = **Refractive Regression** (loss of near vision).

**The Solution (Compensation):**
To defeat this biological smoothing, the surgeon must effectively "over-dig" the trough.
*   **+0.50D Sphere:** Digs the trough *deeper*.
*   **-0.10 Q:** Modifies the *slope* of the trough walls to be more prolate (steeper transition).
*   **Outcome:** The epithelium *still* fills the trough, but because we started deeper, the *final* surface (Stroma + Hyperplastic Epithelium) settles exactly at the desired curvature.

## 2. Proposed Image Prompt (DALL-E 3)

**Objective:** Create a cross-sectional diagram that clearly separates the **Stromal Cut** (Physics) from the **Epithelial Fill** (Biology) across three stages.

**Prompt:**
> A high-precision medical sagittal cross-section diagram of the human cornea, focusing on Hyperopic PRK physiology.
>
> **The image must be divided into three vertical panels (comparative evolution):**
>
> 1.  **Panel 1: "The Physics (Ablation)"**
>     *   Shows the corneal stroma with a deep, precise "ring-shaped trough" cut into the paracentral zone.
>     *   The central cornea remains untouched and stands out as a steep island.
>     *   Label: "LASER ABLATION (Deep Trough)".
>
> 2.  **Panel 2: "The Biology (Regression)"**
>     *   Shows the same stromal shape, but now covered by a semi-transparent purple layer (Epithelium).
>     *   **Crucial Detail:** The epithelium is THICKER inside the trough, filling it in like putty, making the overall surface smoother and flatter.
>     *   Label: "EPITHELIAL MASKING (Loss of Effect)".
>
> 3.  **Panel 3: "The Strategy (Compensation)"**
>     *   Shows a *new* ablation profile that is exaggerated/deeper (indicated by a dashed line comparing to the original).
>     *   The epithelium still fills it, but the *final surface* curvature remains steep and effective.
>     *   Label: "COMPENSATED PROFILE (Stable Result)".
>
> **Style:** Clean schematic vector art, white background, scientific color palette (Cornea = light blue/cyan, Epithelium = purple/magenta). Arrows indicating "Filling" and "Maintained Curvature".
