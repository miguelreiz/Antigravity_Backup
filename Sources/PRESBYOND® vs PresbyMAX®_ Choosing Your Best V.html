What is the Difference Between PRESBYOND® and PresbyMAX®?

  • Posted July 7, 2025
  • Book Your Consultation Now Medically Reviewed by Mr Mfazo Hove Consultant Ophthalmologist
  • Author: Chris Dunnington
  • Published: July 7, 2025 Your guide to advanced laser eye surgery for presbyopia at Blue Fin Vision® Eye Clinic, London Presbyopia, the age-related loss of near vision, is a common concern for adults over forty. For those seeking independence from reading glasses or varifocals, today’s advanced laser eye surgery options offer highly effective solutions. At Blue Fin Vision® Eye Clinic, on Harley Street, we offer two of the most sophisticated procedures available: PRESBYOND® Laser Blended Vision and PresbyMAX®. But what is the difference between these two treatments, and which is right for you? This article explores the key distinctions between PRESBYOND® and PresbyMAX®, guiding you through the “presbymax vs presbyond” debate and helping you make an informed choice with London’s top-rated eye clinic.

Understanding Presbyopia and Modern Laser Eye Surgery

Presbyopia occurs naturally as the eye’s lens loses flexibility, making it difficult to focus on near objects. While glasses and contact lenses are traditional solutions, laser eye surgery for presbyopia provides a minimally invasive, long-lasting alternative that restores clear vision at all distances.

PRESBYOND® Laser Blended Vision: Seamless, Personalised Vision

PRESBYOND® is an advanced, wavefront-optimised LASIK procedure developed by ZEISS to address presbyopia. Unlike conventional monovision, which can leave a “blur zone” at intermediate distances, PRESBYOND® creates a unique “Blend Zone” on the cornea, allowing the brain to merge images from both eyes for:

  • Excellent near, intermediate, and distance vision
  • True binocular vision , reducing the adaptation period and minimising side effects
  • Highly personalised treatment , considering your eye’s optical characteristics and the functional age of your eyes How PRESBYOND® Works:
  • The dominant eye is optimised for distance vision, while the non-dominant eye is set to mild myopia (typically around -1.5 D) for near tasks.
  • The laser creates a smooth gradient of refractive power, eliminating the intermediate “blur zone” seen with standard monovision.
  • Most patients enjoy a rapid recovery and immediate improvement in near vision. Who is Suitable for PRESBYOND®?
  • Individuals aged 40–60 with presbyopia and stable prescriptions
  • Those with healthy eyes and no significant ocular pathology
  • Patients frustrated by the limitations of reading glasses or varifocals
  • Suitable for correcting myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism within certain ranges

PresbyMAX®: Multifocal Laser Eye Surgery for Presbyopia

PresbyMAX® is a state-of-the-art laser eye surgery technique using the SCHWIND AMARIS system. This procedure employs a bi-aspheric, multifocal ablation profile, creating multiple focal points on the cornea to deliver:

  • Central vision for near tasks
  • Intermediate vision for activities such as computer work
  • Peripheral vision for distance tasks This multifocal approach mimics the effect of multifocal contact or intraocular lenses, but with the comfort and safety of a corneal procedure. How PresbyMAX® Works:
  • The cornea is sculpted to create distinct optical zones for near, intermediate, and distance vision.
  • The transition between these zones is smooth, supporting stable, high-quality vision at every distance.
  • The procedure is minimally invasive, with rapid restoration of near vision and ongoing improvement in overall visual quality. Who is Suitable for PresbyMAX®?
  • Patients aged 45 and above with presbyopia
  • Individuals with additional refractive errors (myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism)
  • Those who have previously had laser eye surgery for distance vision and now require near vision correction
  • Patients’ post-cataract surgery who still need reading glasses

PRESBYOND® vs PresbyMAX®: Key Differences

When considering presbymax vs presbyond , it is important to understand the unique features of each treatment. Here is a detailed comparison to help you decide which may be right for you:

  • Technology PRESBYOND® uses wavefront-optimised LASIK technology from ZEISS.
  • PresbyMAX® employs a bi-aspheric, multifocal ablation profile with the SCHWIND AMARIS system.
  • Vision Range PRESBYOND® provides a seamless blend across near, intermediate, and distance vision.
  • PresbyMAX® creates distinct optical zones for near, intermediate, and distance vision.
  • Binocular Vision PRESBYOND® maintains natural binocular vision with its Blend Zone, reducing adaptation time.
  • PresbyMAX® supports binocular vision through a mini-monovision effect, with a slightly longer adaptation period.
  • Customisation PRESBYOND® is highly personalised, taking into account the unique optical characteristics and functional age of your eyes.
  • PresbyMAX® is fully tailored to your prescription, eye health, and lifestyle needs.
  • Suitability PRESBYOND® is ideal for those aged 40–60 with presbyopia and stable prescriptions.
  • PresbyMAX® is suitable for patients aged 45 and above, including those with complex prescriptions or previous eye surgery.
  • Recovery PRESBYOND® offers rapid recovery, with most patients noticing improved near vision within hours.
  • PresbyMAX® delivers swift restoration of near vision, with full adaptation typically achieved within several weeks.
  • Correction of Other Refractive Errors Both procedures can correct myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism, though PresbyMAX® is often used for more complex cases.
  • Clinical Data and Satisfaction PRESBYOND® boasts high patient satisfaction and a low incidence of side effects.
  • PresbyMAX® has a strong clinical track record, with over 12,000 successful procedures worldwide.
